About the Lab

Nicolas Martin improves agricultural decision-making by integrating spatial analytics with geospatial intelligence. His research develops participatory frameworks connecting farmers and agronomists as citizen scientists. He prioritizes open-source tools to democratize access to advanced algorithms and complex data. His program focuses on four core areas: on-farm experimentation for nitrogen optimization, high-throughput phenotyping and crop modeling, system thinking in agriculture, and the digitization of farm management records. As an educator he combines leadership training with open-source statistical resources to support diverse student populations. He serves on the American Society of Agronomy board to advance accessible professional training.

Core Research Areas

On-Farm Experimentation

Optimizing nitrogen management through large-scale, decentralized trials conducted directly on commercial farms.

High-Throughput Phenotyping

Utilizing aerial imaging and remote sensing to rapidly assess plant traits and performance in field conditions.

Crop Modeling

Developing algorithms and simulation models to predict crop performance and improve adaptation strategies.

Systems Thinking

Applying holistic frameworks to solve complex agricultural challenges by analyzing the interactions between agronomic, environmental, and management factors.

Digital Records

Developing systems for the digitization and analysis of farm management records to fuel data-driven agronomy.

Envirotyping

Characterizing growing environments to improve crop adaptation and better understand genotype-by-environment interactions.
